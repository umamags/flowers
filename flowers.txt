### 1) Rosa (Rose)

Arguably the most famous flower in the world. Grown in gardens globally for beauty, fragrance, and commercial use (cut flowers, perfumes).

---

### 2) Tulipa (Tulip)

Extremely popular in temperate regions. Widely cultivated in gardens and commercially grown, especially in Europe and North America.

---

### 3) Helianthus (Sunflower)

Grows in many continents. Known for its large yellow blooms and agricultural value (oil production).

---

### 4) Orchidaceae (Orchids)

One of the largest plant families. Found almost worldwide (especially tropical regions). Extremely diverse.

---

### 5) Lilium (Lily)

Common in gardens across temperate regions. Popular ornamental flower with many hybrid varieties.

---

### 6) Dianthus caryophyllus (Carnation)

Widely grown for cut flowers. Popular in Europe, Asia, and the Americas.

---

### 7) Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum)

Extensively cultivated in Asia, Europe, and North America. One of the most popular decorative flowers globally.

---

### 8) Hibiscus (Hibiscus)

Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Common in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

---

### 9) Narcissus (Daffodil)

Widely grown in temperate climates. Very common in spring gardens worldwide.

---

### 10) Bougainvillea

Extremely common in warm climates. Popular in tropical and Mediterranean regions for landscaping.
